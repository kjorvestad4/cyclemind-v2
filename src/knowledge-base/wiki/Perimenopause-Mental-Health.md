# Perimenopause & Mental Health

> **Related:** [[Menopause-Hormone-Therapy]] · [[PMDD]] · [[Cycle-Tracking-Mental-Health]] · [[Bipolar-Disorder-Reproductive-Context]] · [[ADHD-Reproductive-Transitions]]

---

## Definition
Perimenopause is the **menopausal transition** — the period of hormonal flux leading to the final menstrual period. It typically spans **4–8 years** and is defined by irregular menstrual cycles and fluctuating estrogen levels.

### STRAW Staging
| Stage | Definition |
|---|---|
| Early transition | Cycle variability ≥7 days from previous cycles |
| Late transition | ≥2 skipped cycles; stretches of amenorrhea ≥60 days |
| Menopause | 12 consecutive months without menses |

---

## Average Ages
- Perimenopause onset: ~**47 years**
- Final menstrual period (menopause): ~**51 years** (range 45–55)
- Early menopause: 40–45 years
- Primary Ovarian Insufficiency (POI): <40 years — see [[Fertility-Mental-Health]]

## Mental Health in Perimenopause

### Depression Risk
- Women in **menopausal transition** have **2–4x increased risk** of major depression vs. premenopausal baseline
- Risk is HIGHER in those with:
  - History of depression or PMDD
  - Severe vasomotor symptoms
  - Significant sleep disruption
  - Stressful life events (empty nest, aging parents, marital changes)
- Risk does NOT require prior psychiatric history — first-onset depression common

### Anxiety
- Generalized anxiety and panic symptoms increase during perimenopause
- Often linked to vasomotor symptoms (hot flash → anxiety → insomnia → low mood cascade)
- Distinguish from new panic disorder vs. perimenopausal anxiety

### Cognitive Symptoms
- "Brain fog" — memory complaints, word-finding difficulty, concentration problems
- Usually subjective; objective testing shows modest impairment during transition
- Typically improves after menopause; estrogen therapy may help during transition

---

## Vasomotor Symptoms (VMS) & Mood
- Hot flashes and night sweats affect **up to 80%** of perimenopausal women
- Severe VMS strongly associated with depression, anxiety, and sleep disruption
- **Cascade effect:** night sweats → sleep disruption → mood worsening → worsened VMS perception

---

## Screening
- Screen for depression (PHQ-9) and anxiety (GAD-7) at routine visits
- Assess sleep quality (insomnia is common and treatable)
- Track menstrual cycle changes (CycleMind's cycle log)

---

## Treatment Approach

### For Vasomotor Symptoms (+ Mood Benefit)
- **Menopausal Hormone Therapy (MHT)** — most effective for VMS; see [[Menopause-Hormone-Therapy]]
- SSRIs/SNRIs (venlafaxine, escitalopram) — effective for both VMS and mood
- **Fezolinetant** (NK3 receptor antagonist) — FDA-approved non-hormonal VMS treatment

### For Mood
- SSRIs/SNRIs — first-line for perimenopausal depression and anxiety
- MHT may augment antidepressant response in perimenopausal women
- CBT — effective for depression, anxiety, and menopausal coping
- **Note:** Women with prior PMDD may respond particularly well to MHT for mood

### For Sleep
- CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia) — first-line
- Treating underlying VMS often improves sleep
- Low-dose doxepin or suvorexant as pharmacologic adjuncts

---

## Reproductive Hormone Changes
- **FSH** rises (pituitary trying to drive failing ovaries)
- **Estrogen** fluctuates wildly before declining
- **Progesterone** falls (anovulatory cycles)
- **AMH** falls to near zero
- **Inhibin B** falls

### FSH Testing Note
- Single FSH level is NOT reliable to diagnose perimenopause — too variable
- Clinical diagnosis (symptoms + irregular cycles in age-appropriate woman) is preferred
- FSH useful for POI diagnosis (<40 years) — two elevated readings required
