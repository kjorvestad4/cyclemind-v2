# Postpartum Depression

> **Related:** [[Perinatal-Depression]] · [[Postpartum-Psychosis]] · [[Postpartum-OCD]] · [[Infant-Mental-Health]] · [[Trauma-Reproductive-Health]]

---

## Baby Blues vs. Postpartum Depression

| Feature | Baby Blues | Postpartum Depression |
|---|---|---|
| Onset | Days 3–5 postpartum | Any time in first year |
| Duration | Resolves by 2 weeks | Persists >2 weeks |
| Severity | Mild; self-limiting | Moderate to severe |
| Functional impact | Minimal | Significant impairment |
| Treatment needed | Reassurance, support | Therapy ± medication |

---

## Prevalence & Timing
- Affects **10–15%** of postpartum women globally
- Onset: most commonly in first 3 months; can occur up to 12 months postpartum
- Higher rates in: adolescents, low-income populations, those with prior depression

---

## Symptoms
- Persistent low mood, emptiness, tearfulness
- Inability to enjoy the baby or motherhood
- Excessive worry about the baby's health or survival
- Intrusive thoughts (distinguish from [[Postpartum-OCD]])
- Guilt, worthlessness, shame
- Fatigue disproportionate to sleep deprivation
- Appetite disturbance
- Suicidal ideation (requires immediate evaluation)

---

## Screening
- **EPDS** ≥10 at any postpartum visit → full clinical evaluation
- Screen at: postpartum visit, well-baby checks (1, 2, 4, 6 months)
- PHQ-9 is an acceptable alternative

---

## FDA-Approved Treatments Specific to PPD

### Brexanolone (Zulresso)
- IV infusion over 60 hours in certified inpatient/outpatient setting
- **Mechanism:** Synthetic neuroactive steroid (allopregnanolone analog)
- Rapid onset — improvement within 24–48 hours
- Not for breastfeeding (limited data)

### Zuranolone (Zurzuvae) — oral
- Daily oral pill; 14-day course
- Same neurosteroid mechanism
- Rapid onset; does not require hospitalization
- Breastfeeding: use with caution (some transfer)

---

## Standard Antidepressants in PPD

### SSRIs (First-Line for Moderate-Severe PPD)
| Agent | Breastfeeding Safety | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sertraline | ✅ Preferred | Low infant exposure; most data |
| Paroxetine | ✅ Acceptable | Discontinuation syndrome risk |
| Escitalopram | ✅ Acceptable | Good tolerability |
| Fluoxetine | ⚠️ Use with caution | Long half-life; more infant exposure |

### General Principles
- Start at low dose; titrate after 1–2 weeks
- Full therapeutic effect in 4–6 weeks
- Continue for **at least 6–12 months** after remission
- Do not abruptly discontinue

---

## Psychotherapy
- **CBT and IPT** are evidence-based first-line for mild-moderate PPD
- Can be delivered individually, in groups, or via telehealth
- Equally effective to medication for mild-moderate cases
- Superior to medication alone when combined

---

## Breastfeeding Considerations
- Most SSRIs compatible with breastfeeding at low infant exposure
- Discuss individualized risk-benefit with provider
- Untreated depression poses greater risk to infant than most antidepressants

---

## Impact on Infant & Family
- Impaired mother-infant attachment — see [[Infant-Mental-Health]]
- Child behavioral and developmental concerns
- Partner depression rates increase when maternal PPD present
- Early treatment protects the entire family system

---

## When to Escalate
- Suicidal or homicidal ideation → immediate psychiatric evaluation
- Psychotic symptoms → rule out [[Postpartum-Psychosis]]
- No response to 2 adequate SSRI trials → specialist referral
- Suspected bipolar disorder → see [[Bipolar-Disorder-Reproductive-Context]]
