## The Menstrual Cycle — Mental Health Lens

### Phase Overview

| Phase | Days (28-day cycle) | Hormones | Typical Mood Effects |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Menstrual** | 1–5 | Estrogen & progesterone low → rise | Mood often improves by day 2–3 |
| **Follicular** | 1–13 | Rising estrogen | Improving mood, energy, motivation |
| **Ovulatory** | 13–15 | Estrogen peak; LH surge | Often best mood, energy, libido |
| **Early luteal** | 15–22 | Progesterone rises; estrogen moderate | Generally stable |
| **Late luteal** | 22–28 | Hormone withdrawal | PMDD/PMS vulnerability window |

---

## Why the Luteal Phase Affects Mood

### Serotonin System
- Estrogen upregulates serotonin synthesis and receptor sensitivity
- Progesterone metabolite (allopregnanolone) modulates GABA-A receptors
- Luteal-phase progesterone rise then fall → altered GABA and serotonin signaling → mood vulnerability

### HPA Axis
- Progesterone moderately suppresses the HPA axis
- Withdrawal at menses → cortisol rebounds → anxiety and irritability
- In PMDD: exaggerated HPA reactivity to hormonal changes

## DRSP — Daily Record of Severity of Problems

### What It Is
The **gold-standard validated tool** for prospective PMDD diagnosis and treatment monitoring.

### How It Works
- Daily ratings of 21 symptoms on a 1–6 scale
- Completed for **at least 2 full cycles**
- Documents symptom timing relative to cycle phase

### DRSP Symptom Domains
1. **Affective:** Depressed, hopeless, anxious, mood swings, irritable, angry, interpersonal conflicts
2. **Cognitive:** Difficulty concentrating, out of control, overwhelmed
3. **Physical:** Cramps, bloating, breast tenderness, headache, joint pain, sleep changes, appetite changes
4. **Functional:** Work interference, social interference, relationship interference

### Scoring for PMDD Diagnosis
- ≥5 symptoms with ≥1 affective symptom
- Symptom severity in late luteal phase: average ≥30% higher than follicular phase average
- Minimal/absent symptoms in follicular phase (days 4–12)

## Ovulation Tracking

### Methods
| Method | How It Works | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| OPK (LH test strips) | Detects LH surge 24–36h before ovulation | Fertility timing, cycle characterization |
| BBT (basal body temperature) | Rise of 0.5°F confirms ovulation has occurred | Retrospective confirmation |
| Cervical mucus | Egg-white consistency indicates fertile window | Combined with OPK for accuracy |
| Ultrasound | Most accurate; not practical for daily tracking | Fertility evaluation |

### Fertile Window
- Typically 5–6 days ending on day of ovulation
- Highest fertility: 1–2 days before ovulation
- Sperm viable up to 5 days; egg viable 12–24 hours post-ovulation

## Cycle Length Variation
- **Normal range:** 24–38 days
- **Adolescents:** 21–45 days (irregular for first 1–4 years after menarche)
- **Perimenopause:** Increasing variability; shorter then longer cycles
- Stress, illness, weight change, travel can all alter cycle length
