# Hormonal Contraception & Mental Health

> **Related:** [[PMDD-Overview]] · [[Cycle-Tracking-Mental-Health]] · [[Luteal-Phase-SSRI-Dosing]]

---

## Overview

Hormonal contraceptives significantly alter the neuroendocrine environment. For most women, this is neutral or positive. For a subset — particularly adolescents and those with mood vulnerability — there is meaningful risk. Individualizing contraceptive choice requires considering mental health history.

---

## The Evidence Base

### Danish Cohort Study (Skovlund et al., JAMA Psychiatry 2016)
- **Sample:** 1 million Danish women followed for 13 years
- **Finding:** All hormonal contraceptive types associated with increased first-time antidepressant use and depression diagnosis
- **Highest risk:** Progestin-only methods, adolescents (teens had 2× higher relative risk)
- **Absolute risk increase:** Small but clinically meaningful in high-risk populations
- **Key caveat:** Confounders present; causality not definitively established

### What We Know
- Estrogen generally has **mood-stabilizing effects** via serotonin modulation
- Synthetic progestins can have **mood-dysregulating effects**, especially in susceptible individuals
- **Progestin type matters** significantly (see table below)
- The pill-free interval in cyclic OCP regimens may create a "mini-withdrawal" resembling the luteal phase

## Contraceptive Options by Mood Profile

### Combined Oral Contraceptives (COCs)

| Progestin | Mood Profile | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Drospirenone** (Yaz, Yasmin) | Most favorable — FDA-approved for PMDD | Anti-androgenic, aldosterone-blocking; reduces luteal symptoms |
| **Norethindrone** | Neutral to mild negative | Most widely studied; generally well-tolerated |
| **Levonorgestrel** | Moderate androgenic activity | May worsen mood in some; avoid in PMDD patients |
| **Desogestrel** | Mixed data | Less androgenic than LNG; variable mood effects |
| **Norgestimate** | Generally neutral | Low androgen activity |

### Progestin-Only Methods

| Method | Mood Concern Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Mini-pill (norethindrone)** | Low-moderate | Continuous; no pill-free interval |
| **Depo-Provera (DMPA)** | Moderate-high | High-dose progestin; strongest mood signal in studies; some women thrive |
| **Nexplanon implant (etonogestrel)** | Moderate | High discontinuation due to mood/bleeding; but many women have no issues |
| **Levonorgestrel IUD (Mirena, Kyleena, Liletta)** | Low-moderate | Mostly local effect; low systemic absorption; generally well-tolerated |

### Non-Hormonal Options (Mood-Neutral)
| Method | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Copper IUD (Paragard)** | Fully hormone-free; may worsen menstrual pain/bleeding — relevant for endo/PMDD |
| **Condoms** | Hormone-free; no cycle effects |
| **Fertility Awareness Methods** | Hormone-free; requires consistent tracking |

## Clinical Decision Framework

### Start with Screening
Before prescribing hormonal contraception:
1. PHQ-9 at baseline
2. Ask about personal or family history of depression
3. Ask about history of PMDD or severe PMS
4. Ask about mood response to prior hormonal contraceptives

### For Women with PMDD
- **First choice:** Drospirenone-containing OCP (Yaz 24/4 — FDA-approved for PMDD)
- **Continuous OCP** to eliminate the pill-free interval may work better for some
- **Avoid:** High-androgen progestins (levonorgestrel-dominant pills)
- **Consider:** Copper IUD if OCP mood effects are unacceptable (loses cycle-regulation benefit)

### For Women with Depression History
- Start with the most mood-favorable option: drospirenone or copper IUD
- PHQ-9 at 3 months after starting
- Clear discussion: if mood worsens, contraceptive switch is a valid clinical option
- Do not dismiss mood changes as "not related to the pill" — they may well be

### For Adolescents
- Higher relative risk of mood effects
- Consider non-hormonal options or drospirenone COCs
- More frequent follow-up at 1 and 3 months after initiation
- Open communication with patient about what to watch for

## Monitoring Protocol

| Timepoint | Action |
|---|---|
| Baseline | PHQ-9, mood history, contraceptive preference discussion |
| 1 month | Brief check-in — mood, breakthrough bleeding, tolerability |
| 3 months | Repeat PHQ-9; formal mood review |
| 6 months | If stable, annual monitoring |
| Any time | If PHQ-9 increases ≥5 points or patient reports mood concern, review |
