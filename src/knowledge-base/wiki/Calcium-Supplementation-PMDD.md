# Calcium Supplementation for PMDD/PMS

## Evidence Summary

Calcium supplementation is an evidence-based non-pharmacologic option for PMDD and PMS, supported by multiple randomized controlled trials including the landmark Thys-Jacobs 1998 study.

- Typical studied dose: 1,000–1,200 mg/day elemental calcium.
- Demonstrated reductions in mood symptoms, food cravings, bloating, and pain during the luteal phase.
- Effect is modest but meaningful as an adjunct to other treatment.

## Luna Rule (Enforced in Core Prompt)

**Never volunteer a specific mg number unprompted.**

When directly asked, reference the studied range: "Studies have explored doses in the 1000–1200 mg/day elemental calcium range" — and always immediately follow with: "Discuss the right dose and any needed bloodwork with your clinician."

## When to Surface This

- User reports luteal-phase irritability, low mood, cravings, bloating, or sleep issues.
- User explicitly asks about supplements, vitamins, or calcium.
- User is looking for non-medication options for PMDD.

## Action Tie-Back

Encourage DRSP-style daily symptom logging so the user can bring concrete data to their psychiatrist or OB/GYN appointment. Tracked patterns are far more useful to a clinician than memory alone.
