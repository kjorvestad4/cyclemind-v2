# Schizophrenia & Related Disorders in the Reproductive Context

> **Related:** [[Postpartum-Psychosis]] · [[Bipolar-Disorder-Reproductive-Context]] · [[Perinatal-Depression]]

---

## Overview
Women with schizophrenia can have successful reproductive experiences with **specialized, proactive, multidisciplinary care**. The postpartum period carries elevated relapse risk; proactive management is essential.

---

## Epidemiology & Reproductive Considerations
- Schizophrenia affects **~1%** of the population; roughly equal sex prevalence
- Women with schizophrenia have higher rates of unintended pregnancy (impaired judgment; medication interactions with OCPs)
- Pregnancy outcomes generally worse in untreated illness vs. treated illness

---

## Relapse Risk

### During Pregnancy
- Relatively lower relapse risk during pregnancy vs. postpartum
- **Medication discontinuation** is the primary driver of relapse — do not discontinue antipsychotics without specialist guidance
- Psychotic relapse during pregnancy: serious obstetric and psychiatric emergency

### Postpartum
- **Significantly elevated** relapse risk — especially first 4 weeks
- Sleep deprivation is a major trigger
- Prophylactic continuation of antipsychotic is recommended

---

## Antipsychotic Safety in Pregnancy

### General Principles
- **Benefits of treatment** (preventing relapse, maintaining maternal function) almost always outweigh teratogenic risks
- Abrupt discontinuation during pregnancy carries high relapse risk with serious consequences

### Second-Generation (Atypical) Antipsychotics — Preferred

| Agent | Relative Safety | Special Considerations |
|---|---|---|
| **Quetiapine** | Good data; low EPS | Metabolic monitoring |
| **Olanzapine** | Good data | Higher metabolic risk; gestational diabetes |
| **Risperidone** | Moderate data | Some prolactin elevation |
| **Clozapine** | Use when necessary | Weekly CBC required; neonatal agranulocytosis risk |
| **Aripiprazole** | Limited but reassuring | Low metabolic impact |

### First-Generation (Typical) Antipsychotics
- Haloperidol: Longest safety record in pregnancy; effective; some EPS risk
- Chlorpromazine: More sedation and metabolic effects
- Generally second-line to atypicals

### Monitoring During Pregnancy
- Fetal anomaly scan (20 weeks)
- Gestational diabetes screening (earlier + repeat due to metabolic risk)
- Fetal growth scans
- Neonatal observation: withdrawal, EPS, sedation

---

## Breastfeeding with Antipsychotics

| Agent | Transfer | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Quetiapine | Very low | Generally compatible |
| Olanzapine | Low | Generally compatible; monitor infant |
| Risperidone | Low-moderate | Use with caution |
| Haloperidol | Low | Compatible; monitor |
| Clozapine | Avoid | Risk of infant agranulocytosis |

---

## Parenting with Schizophrenia

### Challenges
- Cognitive disorganization may impair parenting capacity
- Negative symptoms (flat affect, social withdrawal) may reduce responsiveness
- Medication side effects (sedation) can affect parenting
- Custody concerns common; stigma from child protective services

### Supports That Help
- **Home visiting programs** (Nurse-Family Partnership)
- **Parenting skills groups** tailored for psychiatric populations
- **Family/partner involvement** in infant care
- **Advance care planning** for acute episodes

### Custody Considerations
- Having a mental illness alone does not determine parenting fitness
- Stability on medication, insight, and support system are key factors
- Psychiatric team should document stability and support advocacy when needed

---

## Contraception Considerations
- Higher unintended pregnancy rates → proactive contraception counseling essential
- **LARC (IUDs, implant)** preferred — not user-dependent
- Olanzapine: does not affect OCP efficacy
- Clozapine: may raise OCP failure risk (enzyme induction)

---

## Perimenopause
- Estrogen decline may worsen psychotic symptoms
- Dopamine receptor sensitivity changes with estrogen fluctuation
- May need antipsychotic dose adjustments
- MHT may have adjunctive benefit for some women with schizophrenia
