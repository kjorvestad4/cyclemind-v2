# Trauma & Reproductive Health

## Core Principle: Trauma-Informed Care
Reproductive health care must be **trauma-informed**, recognizing that:
- Trauma is highly prevalent in reproductive-age women
- Gynecologic exams, labor, and delivery can reactivate trauma
- Providers who ask sensitively and offer control dramatically improve outcomes

### Five Trauma-Informed Principles (SAMHSA)
1. **Safety** — physical and emotional safety in all encounters
2. **Trustworthiness** — transparency; clear explanations of procedures
3. **Choice** — offer options; avoid language of coercion
4. **Collaboration** — partnership with the patient
5. **Empowerment** — build on strengths; foster resilience

## Types of Trauma with Reproductive Impact

### Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs)
- Physical, emotional, sexual abuse; neglect; household dysfunction
- Dose-response relationship: higher ACE score → higher risk of:
  - Unintended pregnancy
  - STIs
  - Perinatal complications
  - Perinatal mental health disorders
- ACEs linked to epigenetic changes affecting HPA axis reactivity

### Intimate Partner Violence (IPV)
- Affects **1 in 4 women** in their lifetime
- Pregnancy does NOT protect — IPV often increases or begins during pregnancy
- **Perinatal IPV associations:**
  - Depression and PTSD
  - Late entry to prenatal care
  - Preterm birth; low birth weight
  - Substance use
- **Screening:** Routine IPV screening recommended at all prenatal visits (in private)
- **Tools:** HITS (Hurt, Insult, Threaten, Scream); Abuse Assessment Screen

### Sexual Trauma
- History of sexual assault/abuse:
  - Higher rates of gynecologic pain, vaginismus, sexual dysfunction
  - Avoidance of pelvic exams → delayed prenatal care
  - Dissociation during labor/delivery
  - Hyperemesis gravidarum association
- Inform provider of history so exams can be adapted (position choices, pacing, stopping)

### Birth Trauma
- Emergency cesarean, perceived loss of control, feeling unheard, near-death experience
- **Postpartum PTSD** occurs in ~3–6% of women after childbirth; up to 30% in high-risk groups
- Symptoms: flashbacks, nightmares, avoidance of infant reminders, hyperarousal
- Can co-occur with or be mistaken for PPD

## PTSD in the Perinatal Period

### Presentation
- Re-experiencing (flashbacks, nightmares about birth or prior trauma)
- Avoidance (of memories, reminders, infant, medical care)
- Negative cognitions ("I am damaged"; "It was my fault")
- Hyperarousal (startle, sleep disruption, irritability)

### Treatment
- **Trauma-Focused CBT (TF-CBT)** — first-line; highly effective
- **CPT (Cognitive Processing Therapy)** — targets trauma-related beliefs
- **EMDR** — evidence-based for PTSD
- **SSRIs** — sertraline, paroxetine FDA-approved for PTSD; also treat comorbid depression
- Avoid benzodiazepines as primary treatment (interfere with trauma processing)
