# Luteal Phase SSRI Dosing for PMDD

> **Related:** [[PMDD]] · [[Cycle-Tracking-Mental-Health]] · [[Perinatal-Depression]]

---

## Why SSRIs Work for PMDD (Mechanism)
Unlike depression, SSRIs work within **hours to days** for PMDD — not the weeks required for depression. This suggests PMDD response is mediated by **rapid modulation of neurosteroid (allopregnanolone) synthesis** rather than classic serotonin reuptake inhibition.

---

## Dosing Strategies

### 1. Continuous Daily Dosing
- Take SSRI every day regardless of cycle phase
- **Best for:** Women who have difficulty identifying luteal phase onset; comorbid depression/anxiety
- Onset of PMDD relief: often within first cycle

### 2. Luteal-Phase Only (Intermittent) Dosing ⭐ Preferred for Many
- Start SSRI approximately **14 days before expected menses** (cycle day ~14–16)
- Discontinue on **day 1–2 of menses**
- **Advantages:** Lower cumulative medication exposure; avoids side effects in follicular phase; often preferred by patients
- **Disadvantage:** Requires predictable cycle; harder if cycles irregular

### 3. Symptom-Onset Dosing
- Start SSRI when symptoms first appear (typically 5–10 days before menses)
- Flexible but less systematic than calendar-based luteal dosing

---

## Agent Selection & Doses

| SSRI | Starting Dose | Usual Effective Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sertraline** | 25–50 mg | 50–150 mg | Most studied for PMDD; well tolerated |
| **Fluoxetine** | 10–20 mg | 20 mg | FDA-approved as **Sarafem** for PMDD |
| **Escitalopram** | 5–10 mg | 10–20 mg | Good tolerability |
| **Paroxetine CR** | 12.5 mg | 12.5–25 mg | FDA-approved for PMDD; watch CYP2D6 interactions |
| **Citalopram** | 10–20 mg | 20–40 mg | Limited PMDD-specific data |

**Note:** Paroxetine should be avoided in women on tamoxifen (strong CYP2D6 inhibitor).

---

## Efficacy
- SSRIs reduce PMDD symptoms in **60–75%** of patients
- Both continuous and luteal-phase dosing are comparably effective
- Response often apparent within **1–2 cycles**
- If inadequate response after 2 cycles → increase dose before switching

---

## Side Effects to Counsel
- **Most common:** Nausea, headache, insomnia, decreased libido, delayed orgasm
- **Sexual side effects** are less prominent with intermittent dosing
- GI side effects improve after first 1–2 weeks

---

## When to Switch or Escalate
1. No response after 2 cycles at target dose → try a different SSRI
2. Partial response → add non-pharmacologic strategies (CBT, calcium, exercise)
3. SSRI failure → consider OCP with drospirenone, GnRH agonist

---

## Discontinuation
- Intermittent dosing: natural stop at menses onset — no taper needed (short exposure)
- Continuous dosing: taper if stopping (avoid abrupt discontinuation, especially paroxetine)
