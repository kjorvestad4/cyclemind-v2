# PMDD Luteal Phase Management

> **Related:** [[PMDD-Overview]] · [[Luteal-Phase-SSRI-Dosing]]

## Overview

**PMDD** symptoms confined to the late **luteal phase** and resolving post-menses are managed with targeted intermittent pharmacotherapy and supportive interventions. This approach aligns with CycleMind protocols for cycle-specific care.

---

## Key Clinical Points

- Symptoms limited to the late luteal phase with post-menses resolution qualify for **intermittent** treatment.
- **First-line pharmacologic option:** luteal-phase dosing of an **SSRI** (e.g., **sertraline** or **fluoxetine**).
- **Non-pharmacologic interventions:** [[Integrative-Perinatal-Depression|CBT]], regular exercise, sleep hygiene, and **calcium supplementation (1200 mg/day)**.

## Treatment Implications

Intermittent luteal-phase dosing minimizes continuous SSRI exposure and side effects while addressing peak symptoms. This is particularly relevant for patients who:
- Experience side effects at standard continuous doses
- Have mild-to-moderate PMDD limited strictly to the luteal window
- Prefer the psychological framing of cycle-targeted treatment

Combine with lifestyle measures for a **multimodal protocol** — exercise + calcium + sleep hygiene together may rival or approach SSRI efficacy in mild cases. Monitor response across a minimum of 2 full cycles before adjusting.
