Maternal depression is also associated with poor behavioral, emotional, and cognitive 
outcomes for children. It is important to screen pregnant and postpartum women for 
depressive illness and to treat that illness when found. Because psychiatrists are not 
typically educated about the risks of untreated perinatal depression, many physicians 
overestimate the relative risks of using an tidepressant drugs, with the result that 
many women with depression in pregnancy and postpartum are untreated. Mild 
symptoms can respond to nonpharmacological treatments, and most antidepressants 
can be used safely in pregnancy and the postpartum. 
Key Points 
• Depression is common both in pregnancy and postpartum, with an elevated 
biological risk in the early postpartum period. 
• A personal or family history of depression is the strongest single risk factor of 
perinatal depression. 
• Suicide is a leading cause of peripartum mortality, and all decisions about 
treatment in this period must bear that in mind. 
• Although changes in reproductive hormones may be involved in triggering 
postpartum depressive symptoms, little evidence shows that levels of estrogen 
and progesterone differ between women with depression and healthy women; 
rather, some women appear to be vulnerable to shifting hormones. 
• Good screening tools, including the Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale, 
are important but cannot take the place of a clinical diagnosis. 
• Women with mild or moderate symptoms can often be treated successfully with 
psychotherapy and other nonpharmacological approaches, but differences in 
access to care may affect women’s ability to engage in such treatment. 
• Women with moderate to severe symptoms usually require pharmacological 
treatment. 
• Although very few randomized controlled trials have been published, increas-
ingly sophisticated statistical techniques have resulted in rigorous data from 
observational trials over the past few years. 
• Most data indicate that the risks of untreated illness often outweigh the risks 
of medication, so clinicians should no t hesitate to prescribe medication for 
women who are ill, if indicated.
