lists some common contraindications for using combined hormonal contraception. 
The psychiatrist should prescribe contraception if the patient desires it, lacks contrain-
dications, and is likely not pregnant (a useful Quick Start Algorithm for determining 
the likelihood of pregnancy is available from the Reproductive Health Access Project 
at www.reproductiveaccess.org/resource/quick-start-algorithm). The risks of unin -
tended pregnancy generally outweigh the risk of taking oral contraception when al -
ready pregnant. These medications are neit her teratogens nor abortifacients. Their 
main risk when used during early pregnancy is delaying the diagnosis of pregnancy.
