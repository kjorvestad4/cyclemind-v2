139 Infertility and Perinatal Loss 
• Infertility medications may be associat ed with significant mood and anxiety 
symptoms. 
• Most studies of the psychiatric issues in infertility patients include heterosex-
ual cisgender women; more research is needed in diverse populations. 
• Assisted reproductive technology (ART ) is emotionally and physically de -
manding, and ART with third parties—such as surrogates—i s especially chal-
lenging. 
• The American Society for Reproductive Medicine publishes guidelines for 
evaluation and treatment of third-party ART participants; mental health clini-
cians should consult these guidelines. 
• Perinatal loss is a common complication of pregnancy and is divided into loss 
prior to 20 weeks’ gestation, loss after 20 weeks’ gestation, and early and late 
neonatal death. 
• Grief is a common reaction followin g perinatal loss but in some cases can 
evolve into a more complex process with prolonged, intense physical and emo-
tional symptoms. 
• Perinatal loss increases the risk of various psychiatric disorders, and it is pos-
sible that psychiatric illness increases the risk of perinatal loss. 
• Certain risk factors are found in all forms of perinatal loss, including lack of so-
cial support, previous psychiatric disorders, and previous history of trauma. 
• Treatment of the psychological consequences of per inatal loss revolves around 
psychotherapy and psychosocial support for the woman and the couple (where 
applicable).
