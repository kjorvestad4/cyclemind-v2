Conclusion 
PPP is a rare condition that may present with a rapid onset of symptoms early in the 
postpartum period. PPP presents significantly elevated risk to mother and child if not 
swiftly diagnosed and treated. Because mothers with PPP most often require inpa -
tient treatment at initial presentation, careful attention to the mother–baby dyad, fam-
ily system, and maintaining lactation (if desired by the patient) are additional but 
critical components of care. Women with a prior history of PPP and women with bi -
polar disorder are at highest risk of developing PPP and should be treated prophylac-
tically in the postpartum period if not already receiving maintenance medication 
during pregnancy. Although benzodiazepines, antipsycho tics, lithium, and ECT all 
have evidence to support their use in the acute treatment of PPP , lithium has the stron-
gest evidence to support its use in prophylactic treatment.
