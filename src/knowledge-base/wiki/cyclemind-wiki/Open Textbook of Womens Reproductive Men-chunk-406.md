Probiotics 
Probiotics are living microorganisms that may play a beneficial role in health, and the 
use of probiotics during pregnancy to reduce rates of multiple adverse pregnancy out-
comes has become a common study interest. The proposed mechanisms of action of 
probiotics in the mental health literature focus on a bidirectional relationship between 
the brain and gastrointestinal tract (i.e., the “gut-brain axis”). Clear mechanisms of ac-
tion, however, remain the topic of research and are primarily theoretical at this time 
(Noonan et al. 2020). Probiotics are theorized to affect the genitourinary-gastrointesti-
nal tract relationship and to interfere with pathogens and inflammatory cytokines as-
sociated with preterm labor (Reid and Bocking 2003). 
Unfortunately, recent studies have been lackluster regarding probiotics and reduced 
risks of preterm birth, gestational diabetes, small or large for gestational age infants, 
or premature rupture of membranes. Study variables are considerably heterogeneous, 
including timing of intervention, type of probiotic supplemented, stated versus actual 
content of probiotic, and duration of the in tervention, which makes it difficult to in -
terpret the evidence (Jarde et al. 2018). The Probiotics in Pregnancy study sought to 
evaluate the impact of the probiotic Lactobacillus rhamnosus HN001 on pregnancy and 
postpartum outcomes through a large randomized controlled trial. Women who re -
ceived the probiotic had significantly lower Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale 
scores and lower rates of anxiety screening scores than those who received placebo 
(Slykerman et al. 2017). Although effects were modest, L. rhamnosus may be a reason-
able recommendation as an adjunct for mild depression and anxiety, along with stan-
dard-of-care screening and treatment recommendations during pregnancy. 
Melatonin 
Melatonin is a hormone secreted by the pineal gland (and the placenta during pregnancy) 
and is involved in regulating normal sleep-wake cycles by influencing circadian rhythm 
timing. It has also been shown to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antiapoptotic 
effects (Yang et al. 2018). Melatonin supplements are typically created synthetically (al-
though they can be derived from animals or microorganisms) and are sold over the 
counter as a supplement for insomnia and circadian rhythm problems, such as jet lag. 
From existing human and animal data, we understand that the interaction between 
normal circadian rhythms and melatonin pr oduction is important across human re -
production. During human pregnancies, melatonin serum concentrations increase 
markedly, and this is thought to stimulate luteal progesterone production and to de-
crease prostaglandin production (Brzezinsk i et al. 1992). Melatonin both crosses the 
placenta and is produced by the placenta in normal pregnancies (Reiter et al. 2014). 
Melatonin also plays several roles in normal pregnancy, including fetal maturation 
and the onset of labor, in association with oxytocin. Researchers are currently exam-
ining the use of exogenous melatonin in reducing adverse neurodevelopmental out -
