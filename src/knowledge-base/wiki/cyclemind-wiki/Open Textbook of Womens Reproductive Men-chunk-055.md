Conclusion 
Although contraceptive care has not traditionally been within the scope of mental 
health care, the documented interplay between reproductive health and mental health 
clearly indicates that clinical excellence in psychiatric care includes competency in con-
traceptive counseling and management. Psychiatrists and other mental health provid-
ers are well positioned to educate women about the risks and benefits of contraception 
and the ways in which mood symptoms may be influenced by various methods. At a 
minimum, prevention of unwanted pregnancy should be a core component of compre-
hensive psychiatric care.
