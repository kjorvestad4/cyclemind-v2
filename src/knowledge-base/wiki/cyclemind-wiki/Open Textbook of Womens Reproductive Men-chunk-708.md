If thoughts of harming a child are present, one should consider whether they are re-
lated to OCD or due to psychosis. OCD occurring alone has not been demonstrated to 
increase risk of infanticide (Booth et al. 2014). In fact, intrusive thoughts about child 
harm are not uncommon in the postpartum period, and it is crucial that clinicians be 
able to distinguish between intrusive thoughts of harm that are ego-dystonic and hor-
rifying to the mother (i.e., obsessions) and those that are ego-syntonic (i.e., delusions). 
For a complete discussion of OCD, please see Chapter 20, “Obsessive-Compulsive Dis-

In general, marriage is considered a protective factor against suicide, as is being a 
mother to a child under 18. However, one must still consider the risk of both infanticide 
and suicide. Suggestions for prevention of filicide-suicide include appropriate treat-

any suicidal or filicidal thoughts. Community education about symptoms of postpar-
