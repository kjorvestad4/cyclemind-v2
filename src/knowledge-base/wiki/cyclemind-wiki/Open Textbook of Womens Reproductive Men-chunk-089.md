Course and Differential Diagnosis 
VMS vary greatly in severity, with stud ies suggesting that most women experience 
mild to moderate symptoms. VMS last a me an of 5 years but may last substantially 
longer for some women (Col et al. 2009; Freeman et al. 2011). In POAS, a 13-year lon-
gitudinal study involving assessment ev ery 9–12 months of women from premeno -
pause to postmenopause, the most common age at onset for moderate to severe VMS 
was 45–49 years, with an average duration of 10.2 years. Women with moderate to se-
vere VMS onset at a later stage of menopa use had a shorter dura tion of symptoms. 
The median durations were 11.6 years for women who experienced onset in the pre -
and late premenopause stages, 7.4 years for those with onset in the early transition, 
and 3.8 years for those with onset in la te perimenopause or early postmenopause. 
Moreover, Black women experienced a longer duration of VMS than white women in 
this study (Freeman et al. 2011) and in a study by Avis et al. (2015).
