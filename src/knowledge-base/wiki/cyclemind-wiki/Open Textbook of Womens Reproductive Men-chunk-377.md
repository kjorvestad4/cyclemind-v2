Psychosocial Factors 
As covered earlier (see “Risk Factors” and Table 13–1), prior psychiatric history is the 
strongest predictor of the development of PPD, with significant evidence not only for 
MDD but also for anxiety disorders, prior reproductive mood disorders (PPD or pre-
menstrual dysphoric disorder), personality disorders, and any psychiatric disorders (as 
reviewed in Guintivano et al. 2018). This applies both to psychiatric illness that pre-
ceded the pregnancy and to an tenatal illness. Stressful life events or past or current
