Conclusion 
Competent psychiatric care of women with BD across the reproductive lifespan re -
quires the psychiatrist to appreciate the potential influence of fluctuations in female re-
productive hormones. In women of reproductive age, the potential for pregnancy adds 
a layer of complexity to treatment planning. Preconception planning should be a rou-
tine component of psychiatric care for reproductive-age women with BD and should 
include inquiry about plans for pregnancy and contraceptive practices. When prescrib-
ing medications with teratogenic or other obstetrical risk, these risks should be part of 
a balanced risk-risk discussion with the patient or her proxy. When treating BD during 
pregnancy or the postpartum period, it is critical to take a comprehensive approach to 
treatment that includes psychological and pharmacological options and to prioritize 
the need for adequate sleep and social supports. Pharmacological decisions should be
