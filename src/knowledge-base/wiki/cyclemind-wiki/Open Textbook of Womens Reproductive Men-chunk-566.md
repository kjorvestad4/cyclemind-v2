545 Anxiety Disorders and Insomnia in the Perinatal Period 
Key Points 
• Anxiety is one of the most common perinatal mental health symptoms. 
• Perinatal anxiety is associated with numerous adverse outcomes such as pre-
maturity, low birth weight, and decreased head circumference. 
• Perinatal anxiety is an independent risk factor for postpartum depression. 
• Anxiety disorders in pregnancy are treatable; psychopharmacology and psy-
chotherapy are both effective. 
• Benzodiazepines should be used with caution in pregnancy and postpartum. 
• Insomnia in pregnancy is common and should be differentiated from a formal 
anxiety disorder; the two may co-occur. 
• Insomnia is also independently related to adverse outcomes in pregnancy. 
• Cognitive-behavioral therapy for insomnia is an effective first-line treatment.
