We recommend using natural progesterone (vs. synthetic progestins) whenever 
possible, because natural progesterone is metabolized to allopregnanolone, which has 
neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory effects (Yilmaz et al. 2019) and thus may coun-
teract increases in proinflammatory cytokines associated with mood disorders and go-
nadal steroid fluctuations (Hantsoo and E pperson 2015). Due to allopregnanolone’s 
benzodiazepine and barbiturate-like effects at the GABAA receptor, it is recommended 
that women take natural progesterone at bedtime (van Broekhoven et al. 2006). Inter-
estingly, lower levels of allopregnanolone have been associated with depressive symp-
toms during late perimenopause and early postmenopause (Slopien et al. 2018), and 
allopregnanolone in the form of brexanolone has FDA approval for the treatment of 
postpartum depression (Kanes et al. 2017; Meltzer-Brody et al. 2018). In rodent mod-
els, allopregnanolone reverses chronic stress effects on hypothalamic-pituitary-adre-
nal axis function (Evans et al. 2012).
