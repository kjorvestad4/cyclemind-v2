Comprehensive diagnostic interviews have also been developed for adult ADHD, 
both to evaluate diagnostic criteria and to assess various psychopathological syndrome 
scores, functional impairment, indices of pervasiveness, quality of life, and comorbid-
ities (Rösler et al. 2006), such as the Brown Attention-Deficit Disorder Diagnostic Form 
(Brown 1996). The Wender-Reimherr Adult Attention Deficit Disorder Scale (Wender 
1995) is a clinician-rated scal e designed to detect ADHD in adults (Marchant et al. 
2013). Several scales have been specifically designed to assess gender-specific symp -
toms in ADHD, including the ADHD Self-Rating Scale for Women (Nadeau and Quinn 
2005). However, few empirical data exist that would allow for understanding of their 
utility in clinical practice, and no rating scales are specific to the perinatal period. 
Diagnostic Criteria 
The DSM-5 (American Psychiatric Association 2013) diagnostic criteria for ADHD ap-
ply to both childhood and adult ADHD, with no specifier for the perinatal period. 
Three primary subtypes have been identified: predominantly inattentive, hyperactive-
impulsive, and combined presentations (Young et al. 2020). Please see Box 23–1 for the 
DSM-5 diagnostic criteria for ADHD.
