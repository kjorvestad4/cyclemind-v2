Conclusion 
Patients in the perinatal period may be particularly vulnerable to the onset or exacer-
bation of mental health disorders. A number of important clinical emergencies need 
to be identified and treated rapidly and safely. This chapter highlighted several of 
these conditions, including acute suicidality, PPP , agitation, substance intoxication 
and withdrawal, and catatonia. By appreciating the clinical presentation, differential 
diagnosis, and treatment strategies for these emergencies, clinicians can rapidly iden-
tify and manage these conditions to minimize risk of maternal and fetal harm.
