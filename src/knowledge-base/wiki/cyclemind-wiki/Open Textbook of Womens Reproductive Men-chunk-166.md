a favorable balance between efficacy and reassuring safety data for treatment of acute 
episodes of BD during pregnancy. Clinical treatment plans should aim to minimize risk 
after considering an individual patient’s presentation and treatment history. 
In the setting of maintenance treatment for patients who are not acutely symptom-
atic, the severity of the patient’s disease course prior to presentation should be a pri-
mary consideration. A history of inpatient hospitalization, frequent or recent mood 
episodes, psychotic symptoms associated with mood episodes, suicidal ideation or at-
tempts, medication failures, and previous attempts to taper medications should be

favorable reproductive risk profile before or during pregnancy, but any plan to change 
a medication regimen should take into account the anticipated effectiveness of the 
treatment options, the inherent risk of relapse in the setting of medication changes, and, 
in the setting of switching during pregnancy, the risk of multiple medication exposures 
across fetal development.
