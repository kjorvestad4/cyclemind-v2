Conclusion 
Perimenopause is a time of reproductive change that confers increased risk of mental 
illness, as well as complex physical change s such as VMS, difficulty sleeping, sexual 
dysfunction, and cognitive complaints. Distinguishing between depression, the symp-
toms of perimenopause, and other psycholo gical, behavioral, or medical causes of 
overlapping complaints can be challenging but will help guide decisions among the 
many treatment options, which include an tidepressants, hormones, and alternative 
treatments. A growing evidence base is available to guide our understanding and treat-
ment of patients at this time of life.
