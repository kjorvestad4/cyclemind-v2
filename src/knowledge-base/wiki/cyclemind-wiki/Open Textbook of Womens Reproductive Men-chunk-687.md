666 Textbook of Women's Reproductive Mental Health 
• Optimal treatment of ADHD includes a combination of medication and psy -
chosocial support. 
• Stimulants are one of the most commonly prescribed medication in pregnan-
cy. An increasing number of women of reproductive age have been diag -
nosed with ADHD and prescribed stimulants for this condition. 
• Data regarding the risks of stimulant exposure in pregnancy and breastfeed-
ing historically have contained significant confounding variables, such as the 
impact of ADHD itself and substance misuse. 
• Data are still evolving regarding the reproductive safety profile of stimulants; 
much of their effect appears to be related to fetal growth and placental function.
