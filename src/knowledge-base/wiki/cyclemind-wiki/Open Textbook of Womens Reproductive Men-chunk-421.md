Bipolar disorder (BD) is a common psychiatric illness often associated with signifi-
cant impairments in quality of life and psychosocial functioning (Yatham et al. 2018). 
BD frequently presents in adolescence or early adulthood, coinciding with peak repro-
ductive years. Although the sex ratio of BD is roughly equal, there are distinctions in 
the phenomenology of the illness between women and men. The typical age of onset is 
later in women than in men, and women with BD may experience symptom exacerba-
tion in accordance with menstrual cycle hormonal fluctuations, in the perinatal period, 
and in the menopausal transition (Freeman and Gelenberg 2005). BD is a chronic illness 
that typically requires ongoing management during both acute episodes and periods 
of maintenance.
