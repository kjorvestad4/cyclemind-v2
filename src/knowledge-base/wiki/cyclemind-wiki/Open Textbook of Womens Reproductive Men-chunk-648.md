Clinical Features 
Clinical Presentation 
Although the diagnostic criteria for EDs focus mainly on the behaviors and psycho -
logical symptoms surrounding these behaviors, weight, and body image, EDs are also 
associated with medical symptoms and complications. These physical characteristics 
are sometimes more apparent to others and can be the trigger to providers to evaluate 
for an ED. Table 22–3 outlines the symptoms associated with the three most common 
ED diagnoses. 
Anorexia Nervosa
