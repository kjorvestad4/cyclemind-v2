Figure 15–1 provides a useful algorithm to guide decision 
making around maintenance treatment prior to and during pregnancy. 
Once the decision is made to use a medication during pregnancy, awareness of preg-
nancy-related pharmacokinetic changes and the potential need for dosage adjustments 
is an important component of care that may further reduce risk of relapse and avoid 
unintentional supratherapeutic dosing. Therapeutic drug monitoring can complement 
close clinical monitoring. For medications without a standardized therapeutic range, a 
woman’s individual level at a point of euthymia will allow the physician to establish a 
target medication level throughout and after pregnancy (Deligiannidis et al. 2014).
