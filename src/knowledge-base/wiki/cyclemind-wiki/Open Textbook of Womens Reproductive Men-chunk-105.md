Key Points 
• The first postpartum months—the “fourth trimester”—are a time of significant 
psychiatric vulnerability. 
• Decision-making around infant feeding must balance benefits to the infant 
with the mother’s mental health and well-being. 
 
 
 
 
  
 
   
    
  
 
  
 
   
   
  
  
 
 
 
 
 
249 Diagnosis and Treatment in the Postpartum Period 
• Sleep deprivation in new parents is nearly universal; has consequences for 
cognitive, physical and emotional well- being; and is arguably the most im -
portant modifiable risk factor for postpartum psychiatric disorders. 
• Existing health care disparities are exacerbated during this vulnerable time 
and serve as a barrier to quality and timely care for minority women and their 
babies. Improved and more equitable screening and access to care for all is 
imperative to begin to address these disparities.

The perinatal period is a particularly vulnerable time for the emergence or relapse of 
mental health disorders, some of which are associated with clinical emergencies. Health 
care providers, however, may be hesitant to treat emergencies in the perinatal popula-
tion because of uncertainty about managing clinical issues, such as the use of psycho-
tropic medications, in women who are pregnant or breastfeeding. In this chapter, we 
review some of the most common emergencies in reproductive psychiatry: suicidality,
