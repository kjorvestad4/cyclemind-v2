Key Points 
• Obsessions are common in the perinatal period. 
• Perinatal obsessions can include intrusive thoughts of harm coming to the 
child, but having such thoughts does not necessarily mean the mother is at 
risk of harming her child. 
• Distinguishing between ego-syntonic and ego-dystonic thoughts is the key to 
understanding the risk of harm.
