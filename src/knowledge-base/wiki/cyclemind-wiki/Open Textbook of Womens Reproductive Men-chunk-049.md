Physiological Effects of Ovulation Suppression 
Suppression of ovulation via systemic contraception causes distinct changes to female 
sex hormones. During a physiological cycle, levels of estradiol fluctuate across the 
month. During a cycle when a woman is usin g systemic contraception, levels of es-
tradiol are low and relatively constant, mimicking those seen in early folliculogenesis 
(prior to the maturation of a dominant follicle) but much higher than those that occur 
in postmenopausal women (Mis hell et al. 1972). The stabil ization of estradiol levels
