Lithium 
Although historically, experts have recommended against breastfeeding while taking 
lithium, current data suggest that breastfeeding can be a safe option for women receiv-
ing lithium therapy who have a full-term infant, can readily adhere to frequent blood 
draws, and are capable of close monitoring of their infant’s behaviors and alertness in 
collaboration with a pediatrician. Exposure to lithium through breastmilk is substan-
tially less than via placental transfer. Reports of the RID are variable, ranging from 10% 
to 30%, but are generally higher than the accepted 10% value considered to be low risk 
(Newmark et al. 2019; Poels et al. 2018). Although numerous reports are available of 
infants breastfed by women receiving lithium therapy without signs of toxicity or ad-
verse effects (Bogen et al. 2012; Moretti et al. 2003), data do suggest that lithium expo-
sure through breastfeeding can cause adverse effects in cases of dehydration, 
prematurity, or impaired renal function. When lithium is prescribed to a breastfeeding 
woman, collaboration with and close monitoring by the pediatrician is recommended.
