Chapter 17, “Schizophrenia and Related 
Disorders,”) (Epstein et al. 2014). For acute bipolar depression during pregnancy, lam-
otrigine is often considered a preferred agent due to its reassuring safety profile and 
evidence for efficacy in bipolar depression in the general population (Epstein et al. 
2014). Quetiapine has efficacy to support its use in acute bipolar depression, as well as 
a limited but reassuring safety profile in pregnancy (Huybrechts et al. 2016). Although 
lurasidone has evidence to support its use in bipolar depression in the general popu-
lation, data regarding its safety in pregnancy are limited relative to many second-gen-
eration antipsychotics.
