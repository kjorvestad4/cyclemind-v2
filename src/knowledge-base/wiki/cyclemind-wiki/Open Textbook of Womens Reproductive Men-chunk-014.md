Menopause 
Menopause is defined as the absence of menstrual periods for a full year, and it is caused 
by reduced secretion of ovarian hormones, including estrogen and progesterone (Ba-
con 2017). An estimated 1 billion women worldwide have experienced menopause 
(Hoga et al. 2015). The average age of meno pause in the United States is 51.5 years 
(Hoffman et al. 2020).
