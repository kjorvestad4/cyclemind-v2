# Eating Disorders in the Perinatal Period

> **Related:** [[Perinatal-Depression]] · [[Substance-Use-Perinatal]] · [[Trauma-Reproductive-Health]] · [[Integrative-Perinatal-Depression]]

---

## Overview
Eating disorders (EDs) are serious, potentially life-threatening conditions with significant maternal and fetal risks when they occur during pregnancy. They are often under-disclosed due to shame and require a compassionate, non-judgmental approach.

---

## Prevalence
- EDs affect **~5–7%** of women of reproductive age
- Many in partial remission; pregnancy-related body changes can trigger relapse
- Underreported — routine prenatal screening recommended

---

## Types and Perinatal Risks

### Anorexia Nervosa (AN)
- **Defining features:** Restriction of energy intake; intense fear of weight gain; disturbed body image
- **Perinatal risks:**
  - Fetal growth restriction / intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR)
  - Low birth weight
  - Preterm birth
  - Maternal cardiac complications (arrhythmia from electrolyte imbalance)
  - Higher cesarean rate
  - Perinatal mortality risk
- Fertility: Often impaired due to hypothalamic amenorrhea; pregnancy can occur in partial recovery

### Bulimia Nervosa (BN)
- **Defining features:** Recurrent binge-purge cycles; self-evaluation based on weight/shape
- **Perinatal risks:**
  - Electrolyte imbalances (hypokalemia → cardiac arrhythmia)
  - Esophageal tears (Mallory-Weiss)
  - Higher rates of gestational complications
  - Postpartum depression
  - Miscarriage risk elevated

### Binge-Eating Disorder (BED)
- **Defining features:** Recurrent binge eating without compensatory behaviors
- **Perinatal risks:**
  - Excessive gestational weight gain
  - Gestational diabetes
  - Large for gestational age (LGA) infant
  - Higher postpartum depression rates

### ARFID & Other Specified EDs
- May present with severe nutritional deficiencies in pregnancy
- Require multidisciplinary nutritional support

---

## Screening in Pregnancy
Brief validated questions to incorporate into prenatal care:
1. "Do you have any concerns about your eating behaviors or relationship with food?"
2. "Are you worried about your weight or shape?"
3. "Do you ever eat in a way that feels out of control?"
4. "Do you ever restrict eating, skip meals, or try to compensate after eating?"

**SCOFF questionnaire** — 5-question validated screen; ≥2 positive = further evaluation

---

## Treatment Approach

### Core Principles
- **Multidisciplinary team:** OB, perinatal psychiatrist, dietitian, therapist
- **Nutritional rehabilitation** — medical monitoring of weight, electrolytes, fetal growth
- **Mental health treatment** — CBT adapted for pregnancy

### Psychotherapy
- **CBT** (adapted) — first-line for BN and BED
- **Family-Based Treatment (FBT)** — for adolescents with AN
- **DBT** — effective for BED and emotional dysregulation
- **IPT** — effective for BED

### Pharmacotherapy
- SSRIs: effective for BN and BED; limited in AN
- **Fluoxetine** — FDA-approved for BN (weigh risks in pregnancy)
- Avoid: topiramate (teratogenic), naltrexone (limited data), bupropion (seizure risk in AN)

### Hospitalization Indications
- Medically compromised (electrolyte imbalance, cardiac arrhythmia, hypoglycemia)
- Severe malnutrition threatening fetal growth
- Suicidal ideation
- Failed outpatient treatment

---

## Postpartum Considerations
- **Body image:** Postpartum body changes can trigger ED relapse
- Breastfeeding: Nutritional demands may be stressor; discuss openly
- **Sleep deprivation** exacerbates ED symptoms and mood
- Higher rates of PPD in women with ED history

---

## Nutritional Monitoring in Pregnancy
- Monthly weight gain tracking (provider-led, not patient-driven)
- Electrolytes if purging suspected
- Serial growth ultrasounds for fetal monitoring
- Folic acid, iron, omega-3 supplement support
