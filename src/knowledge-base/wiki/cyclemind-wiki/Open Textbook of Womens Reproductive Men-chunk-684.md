Bupropion 
Although bupropion is not considered as effective as stimulants for the symptoms of 
ADHD, it may be a suitable  a l t e r n a t i v e  f o r  s o m e  w o m en (Faraone and Glatt 2010; 
Mészáros et al. 2009; Peterson et al. 2008), and it may have specific advantages, partic-
ularly for women with comorbid depression or women who desire pharmacological 
support for smoking cessation (Baker and Freeman 2018; Freeman 2014). 
Early pregnancy. Overall, more data support the reproductive safety of bupropion 
in pregnancy than support stimulants, although there is less information as compared 
with, for example, selective serotonin reup take inhibitors. There has been concern 
about an association with bupropion exposure and cardiovascular malformations; for 
example, one retrospective case control study using data from a birth defect registry 
noted a small increase in the relative risk of cardiovascular left ventricular outflow de-
fects (with an absolute risk calculated to be  2 out of 1,000 pregnancies) (Alwan et al. 
2010). However, overall studies have been reassuring. For example, prospective reg-
istry data found that the birth defect rate with bupropion exposure in the first trimes-
ter was 3.6%, similar to that found in the unexposed population, and there was no 
clear pattern of birth defects (Bupropion Pregnancy Registry 2008). 
Another prospective study found no eviden ce of a higher rate of malformations 
with bupropion exposure as compared with groups with other antidepressant expo -
sure or no exposure (Chun-Fai-Chan et al. 2005). Furthermore, a large epidemiological 
study of more than 150,000 women compared women with bupropion exposure in the 
first trimester with bupropion exposure in the second trimester and exposure to an -
other antidepressant in the first trimester; no increase in birth defects with first trimes-
ter bupropion exposure was noted (Cole et al. 2007). A recent systematic review aimed 
to consolidate these findings; this review supported the finding of a small increase in 
relative risk of cardiac malformations, al though the absolute risk was low, and con -
founders such as the indication for smo king cessation may have  contributed (Hen -
drick et al. 2017). One small study noted an elevated risk of miscarriage with 
bupropion exposure as compared with a control group; however, the rate remained 
within that of the general population (Chun-Fai-Chan et al. 2005; Hendrick et al. 2017). 
Obstetrical outcomes. Data about other pregnancy or delivery outcomes with bu-
propion exposure are scarce. One small study of 30 pregnant women taking bupropion 
for smoking cessation showed no statistical differences in birth weight, Apgar score, 
or length of gestation in exposed infants (Nanovskaya et al. 2017).
