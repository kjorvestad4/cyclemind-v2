Lithium is one of the most established treatments in BD, with efficacy in all phases 
of the disease and recent evidence supportin g its efficacy in prophylaxis of postpar -
tum mood episodes (Bergink et al. 2012; Yatham et al. 2018). Although early research 
emphasized the potential for teratogenicity with lithium, growing evidence regarding 
its safety profile (reviewed in detail later) supports its use in women who are unlikely 
to achieve or maintain euthymia with lamotrigine or antipsychotic medication mono-
therapy. Although valproate is a foundation of treatment in general for BD, especially 
for mixed states, it is rarely considered a treatment option prior to or during preg -
nancy due to its high teratogenic risk and considerable concern about neurodevelop-
mental outcomes in exposed infants (Yatham et al. 2018).
