Differential Diagnosis 
Even when presenting during times of reproductive events, BD is diagnosed based on 
a thorough psychiatric history. The differen tial diagnosis includes unipolar depres-
sion, especially anxious phenotype, as well as other illnesses with some overlapping 
mood symptoms, including PTSD and borderline personality disorder. Additionally, 
intoxication or withdrawal from substances such as cocaine or alcohol can mimic affec-
tive episodes, and therefore substance use disorders should also be considered. Aside 
from psychiatric illness, many medical illnesses (e.g., thyroid disorders) and medica-
tions (e.g., steroids) may also cause mood symptoms and therefore should be included
