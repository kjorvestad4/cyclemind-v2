Pathophysiology 
Generally, clinically significant anxiety symptoms are thought to arise from a combi-
nation of an inherent biological vulnerab ility and an interaction with a stressful or 
traumatic environmental situation (Domhard t et al. 2019). Anxiety is not a unitary 
phenomenon, as evidenced by the multitude of DSM-5 anxiety disorders and the fact 
that anxiety can be a core feature of ot her syndromes and disorders, ranging from 
OCD to alcohol withdrawal to dementia  (Robinson et al. 2019). In addition, fear—a 
rapid “flight or fight” behavioral response  to an imminent threat—is thought to be 
distinct from anxiety—a lasting state of apprehension about a perceived future threat 
(Robinson et al. 2019). This suggests that, for example, the pathological fear in specific 
phobia and the diffuse worry of GAD may have at least somewhat distinct etiologies 
(Robinson et al. 2019).
