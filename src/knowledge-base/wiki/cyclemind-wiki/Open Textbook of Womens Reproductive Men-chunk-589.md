Stress Response System 
Several studies have examined dysregulation of the stress response system in general 
OCD, with mixed results. There is some consensus that the circadian pattern is un -
changed, but people with OCD exhibit a higher basal level of cortisol than healthy 
control subjects (Kluge et al. 2007; Monteleone et al. 1994). In a small pilot study, Lord 
et al. (2011a) compared the response to a psychological stressor (cold pressor test) be-
tween women with postpartum OCD and healthy postpartum control subjects and 
found that women with OCD had higher basa l salivary levels. Although their levels 
remained higher throughout the stressor, they did not as a group show a greater stress 
response than the healthy women (either by cortisol or by salivary alpha amylase), in-
dicating that perhaps some women with postpartum OCD have a delayed or exagger-
ated stress response. Women with OCD were also more stressed and anxious overall 
and rated the stressor test higher in subjective stress than did healthy control subjects. 
Thus, evidence does not now exist for an overall altered stress response system in 
perinatal OCD but, rather, for an alteration that may affect only a subset of women. 
Thyroid Hormone and Oxytocin 
No studies have examined the role of thyroid dysregulation in perinatal OCD. There 
is evidence of thyroid dysfunction in children and adolescents with general OCD (Mc-
Cracken and Hanna 2005), in individuals with anxiety and trauma-related disorders 
(Friedman et al. 2005; Ittermann et al. 2015), and in PPD and PPP (Bergink et al. 2011, 
2018; Dama et al. 2016; Pedersen et al. 2016 ; Wesseloo et al. 2018), making this a ripe 
area for research in perinatal OCD. Some studies have looked at the role of oxytocin, 
but the data are limited, and results are mixed. Some animal studies have associated 
elevated oxytocin with fewer compulsive-like behaviors (Mitra et al. 2017), and one 
human study found that OCD subjects had higher overall oxytocin levels than healthy 
control subjects (but that within the OCD group, lower oxytocin was associated with
