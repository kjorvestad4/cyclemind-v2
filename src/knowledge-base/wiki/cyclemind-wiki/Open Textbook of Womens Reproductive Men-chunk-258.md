Bulimia Nervosa 
BN is characterized by episodes of binge eating and compensatory purging behaviors 
(American Psychiatric Association 2013). Purging behaviors may include self-induced 
vomiting, laxative abuse, diuretic or emetic abuse, fasting, or excessive exercise. Pa -
tients with BN are usually in the normal and overweight range, are less likely to have 
disruption of the HPG axis, and tend to have preserved sexual function (Cardwell 
2013). Given the recurrent purging, these patients are at risk of dental erosion, caries, 
fractures, gastric or esophageal tears, vomiting-related hypokalemia, and hypochlor-
emic alkalosis (Sadock and Sadock 2007). In contrast to women with AN, these women 
are characterized by impulsivity and lack of control (Cardwell 2013) and have high co-
morbidity of mood and impulse-control disorders (Sadock and Sadock 2007).
