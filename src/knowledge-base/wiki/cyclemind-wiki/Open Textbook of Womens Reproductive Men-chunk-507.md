Reproductive Loss/Traumatic Delivery 
Risk factors specific to reproductive health include trauma with reproductive loss (Ta-
ble 18–2) and traumatic birth experiences (Table 18–3). Reproductive loss includes mis-
carriage, stillbirth, and perinatal or neon atal death. Additionally, some women may 
experience trauma associated with infertility and associated medical procedures, such 
as in vitro fertilization. Traumatic birth experiences include a wide range of medical com-
plications during labor and delivery, poor communication from medical staff, and a 
lack of privacy or dignity, which can lead the mother to fear for the safety of the infant 
or herself. People who witness a partner’s traumatic birth experience are also at risk 
of developing PTSD or other psychiatric di sorders. As with all forms of potentially 
traumatic experiences, whether such an event is perceived as a trauma is subjective and 
depends on a woman’s personal experience, including, significantly, her history of ear-
lier adversity and trauma.
