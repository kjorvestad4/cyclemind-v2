Summary 
Perinatal loss is associated with many psychological and psychiatric reactions in 
women. Most women experience grief reactions, and due to the unexpected nature of 
perinatal loss, many also develop acute stress disorder and PTSD. MDD is especially 
common in women who have experienced recurrent miscarriages and in women with 
IUFD or pregnancy loss after 20 weeks’ gest ation.  Mental health practitioners can 
play a critical role in helping women recover from the despair associated with the loss 
of a pregnancy through psychotherapy and th e use of reproductive loss–related in -
formed grief counseling.
