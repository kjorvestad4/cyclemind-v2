Chapter 9. 
First-Generation Antipsychotics 
Haloperidol and chlorpromazine have the most robust (although still limited) data re-
garding use in lactation. Both medications are secreted into breastmilk in small amounts. 
Limited data suggest no adverse effects in infants exposed to haloperidol monotherapy. 
Sedation was noted in some infants exposed to chlorpromazine through lactation. Al-
though reports of EPS as a result of lactation exposure are rare, they remain a theoretical 
risk in infants exposed to FGAs (Iqbal et al. 2005; Smith and Dubovsky 2017).
