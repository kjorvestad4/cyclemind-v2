When it comes to anxiety and reproductive mental health, as a field we still have 
more questions than answers. On the one hand, anxiety disorders are the most com -
mon mental health conditions in women ov erall (Gregory et al. 2020), and anxiety 
disorders complicate pregnancy and the po stpartum period at a rate higher than 
depressive disorders—affecting up to one-fifth of all pregnancies (Dennis et al. 2017; 
Matthey et al. 2003). In addition, perinata l anxiety has been shown to be associated 
with adverse maternal–infant outcomes, comparable with—and perhaps even exceed-
ing—the impact of perinatal depression (Grigoriadis et al. 2013). Nevertheless, perina-
tal anxiety is underdiagnosed and undertreated, and understanding of the clinical 
features, risk factors, pathophysiology, and treatment remains limited (Fallon et al. 
2016b; Goodman et al. 2014a; Harrison et al. 2020; Misri et al. 2015). However, clinical 
and research interest in this area has increased given both its high prevalence and its 
clear impact on maternal and infant outcomes (Grigoriadis et al. 2018).
