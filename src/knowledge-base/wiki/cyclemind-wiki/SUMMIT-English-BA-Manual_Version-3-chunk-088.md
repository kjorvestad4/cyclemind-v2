Activity Scheduling. Make a specific plan for when, where, and for how long you will do the chosen 
activity. Anticipate barriers that might get in the way or doing the activity when you have planned. 
Consider the ways that challenging logistics, emotions, and thoughts may get in your way. Make a plan 
for how to overcome those barriers.

Take a 30-minute walk during my 
lunch break one time per week. 
Night before the walk, pack a healthy  
snack and water bottle; lay out  
comfortable clothes. 
Scope out a walking route on Tuesday evening that 
will take approximately 30 minutes and is pleasant. 
Ask co-worker/friend to go on walks with me and be an 
accountability partner.  Have a back-up co-worker/friend in  
mind if she says no. 
Bring pair of walking shoes to work to keep in the office on Monday 
morning. 
Review work schedule for the week on Sunday evening and block out time in 
calendar to take a walk. Commit to not scheduling anything else during this time.

COPE: A PROBLEM SOLVING TOOL 
Instructions 
COPE highlights the specific steps that you can take to start an active problem solving approach in your 
life.

1. CLARIFY 
Start by choosing one problem, then clarify what 
exactly the problem is.  Write down the one 
problem and the facts. What about this one 
problem is stressful, challenging, or difficult? 
Also, ask yourself, is this a problem that I have 
control over? If not, you may not be able to solve 
this one; consider redefining the problem as one 
over which you have some control.
