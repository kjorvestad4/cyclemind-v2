c. SHARE COMMUNICATION SKILLS OF ASKING FOR HELP AND SAYING NO: FIRM 
Asking for help and saying no to other people’s requests or demands can support a patient in 
addressing challenges or in getting support for keeping on track with 
her action plans 
1. Explore how easy or difficult it is for the patient to ask for what 
she wants and to set limits or say no to other people’s requests or 
demands on her time 
 Patients vary widely in their comfort with communicating this 
way. Knowing this about the patient can help guide your 
approach to sharing these skills as a BA Treatment Provider 
 Note: Remember to validate that this is hard for many patients. Even if someone is 
generally effective in doing this, some topics or people can be harder than others, or 
sometimes it can be harder to do when feeling down

2. Tell her that asking for help and saying no are skills that can be learned and strengthened 
over time with practice
