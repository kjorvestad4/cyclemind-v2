Psychiatrists and other mental health clinicians treating women during the peri-
natal period have a unique opportunity to assess infant mental health and to provide 
early intervention during a sensitive developmental period. In the prenatal and imme-
diate postpartum periods, a complex interplay between biological and environmental 
factors sets the stage for the offspring’s lifelong physical, neurocognitive, and social-
emotional development. The quality of the main caregiving relationships is among the 
most influential environmental factors in infancy and early childhood. Infants grow in 
an interpersonal context, and they start to learn about themselves and the world they 
live in via their daily interactions with their primary caregivers. Of note, throughout 
this chapter we use the terms primary caregivers and parents interchangeably, although 
we realize that parents are not always the primary or the only primary caregivers for 
infants and young children.
