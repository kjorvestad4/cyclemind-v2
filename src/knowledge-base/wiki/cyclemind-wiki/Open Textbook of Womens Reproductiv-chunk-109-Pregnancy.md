Treatment During Pregnancy,” the risk-risk analysis of medications in pregnancy and 
postpartum is complicated by the limitations of the data, because no gold-standard 
randomized controlled trials of medications in pregnancy or breastfeeding are avail-
able. Instead, data rely on observational studies, which are subject to significant bias 
and confounding (Howland 2013). In addition, the risks of exposure to a medication 
in pregnancy and postpartum must be weighed against the known risks of untreated 
perinatal anxiety.
