Key Points 
• Sexual functioning concerns in women are underassessed and underreported. 
• Female sexual response is determined by a multitude of biopsychosocial vari-
ables and includes domains of desire, arousal, lubrication, and orgasm. 
• Female sexual dysfunctions (FSDs) are classified as a persistent, distressing 
impairment in one or more specific domains of sexual response, or the expe-
rience of pain during sexual activity. 
• Specific biopsychosocial variables associated with reproductive milestones 
may affect women’s sexual functioning. 
• Universal screening for sexual concerns is recommended. 
• Evidence-based approaches to treating FSDs exist, and treatment for FSDs 
is often approached in an interdisciplinary manner.
