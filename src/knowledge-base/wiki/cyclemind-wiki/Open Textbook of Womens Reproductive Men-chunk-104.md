Conclusion 
The fourth trimester is a time of exquisite vulnerability for women due to the physical 
and emotional stress of delivering and caring for a newborn while establishing a new 
identity as a mother and managing a changing role in the family. Breastfeeding can be 
helpful or hurtful, depending on several factors, including how easily the process 
works, dietary problems, medical and medication issues, and managing sleep. Sleep 
deficiencies due to maternal hormonal ch anges and the baby’s nocturnal needs sig -
nificantly increase the possibility of depression and anxiety at a time when women 
are already at risk. The disparities that exist across health care systems are exacerbated 
during this time and further disadvantage minority women and their offspring, with 
long-lasting consequences for these individuals, their families, and their communities. 
We must do better. Women’s mental health presents clear examples of the need for 
a comprehensive health care system that op timizes quality, promotes access, and re-
duces the vast health care disparities of our society.
