Limited human data, mostly registry. No 
known teratogenicity. Lack of sexual side 
effects and weight gain makes it appealing 
to many postpartum women. Also useful 
for smoking cessation. Decreases seizure 
threshold and should thus not be used in 
patients with preeclampsia. 
Limited human data (case reports only). No

Fewer sexual side effects than many other 
antidepressants; works as an antiemetic, so 
useful in hyperemesis gravidarum (al-
though research base is limited) or in pa-
tients in need of weight restoration. 
Low levels in breast milk, in most case

Recent data suggest lithium may have an as-
sociation with cardiac or overall malforma-
tions, but this risk occurs at a much lower 
magnitude than previously postulated (cu-
mulatively reported at OR <2) and is likely 
dose dependent, with those taking >900 mg 
at higher risk. Some risk of infant diabetes 
insipidus leading to polyhydramnios. Rare 
association with newborn decreased mus-
cle tone and initial difficulty breathing and 
feeding. Clearance of lithium may increase 
by 30%–50% during pregnancy. Lithium 
levels should be checked regularly through-
out pregnancy, proximate to delivery, and 
anytime the patient is at risk for dehydra-
tion (e.g., hyperemesis gravidarum, pro-
longed labor) or has impaired renal 
function (e.g., preeclampsia). Regular mon-
itoring of kidney and thyroid function is 
recommended. In the event of incremental 
increases during pregnancy, lithium 
should be restarted at pre-pregnancy dos-
age after delivery. High-resolution ultra-
sound and fetal cardiography 
recommended at 16–20 weeks’ gestation. 
Breastfeeding complicated by risk of 
lithium toxicity in dehydrated 
infants but can be managed with 
pediatrician vigilance and guidance. 
Serum lithium levels in the 
breastfed infants of mothers taking 
lithium have ranged from 10% to 
50% of simultaneous maternal 
serum lithium levels. One 
systematic review recommended 
monitoring of infant lithium, 
thyroid stimulating hormone, and 
renal function tests if clinically 
indicated.
