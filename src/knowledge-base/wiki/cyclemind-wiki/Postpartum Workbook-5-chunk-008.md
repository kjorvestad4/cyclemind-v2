F I N A L  T H O U G H T S
It is important to know that the postpartum period
is not 6-8 weeks as it is typically defined in the
medical system strictly for the purposes of
physical recovery but a rather long one! In fact,
psychologically speaking, it is considered 1 year.
However, many women describe not feeling like
themselves until 1.5-2 years later. It is also
important to keep in mind the hormonal
fluctuations that happen not only during
pregnancy and postpartum but also in the weaning
process and re-emergence of menstruation
(women go through an awful lot huh!). These
transitions are all vulnerable times psychologically
speaking due to hormonal fluctuations. If you
breastfeed beyond a year, you could argue that
until you wean and begin menstruating again you
are still in the postpartum period. 
The postpartum period is a big one, for you, your
relationship, and your family. Because of the lack
of support in the U.S., maternal mental health and
relationships suffer, families suffer. The sad reality
is that we don’t have a village, so do your best to
put one together with whatever resources you
have!
 And know that whatever you do to take care of
yourself, is not selfish, it is an investment not
only in yourself, but in the wellbeing of your
family!
