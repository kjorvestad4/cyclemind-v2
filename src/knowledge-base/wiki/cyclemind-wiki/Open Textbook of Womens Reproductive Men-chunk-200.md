TABLE 18–3. Risk factors associated with developing PTSD following traumatic 
birth experience 
Predisposing Antenatal psychiatric disorders (especially anxiety, depression, or 
history of dissociation), previous trauma, high fear of delivery/ 
pain, first delivery 
Perinatal Subjective distress during delivery, dissociation, loss of control, 
emergency cesarean section, neonatal complications 
Postdelivery Perception of care received as incompetent/uncaring (likely the 
greatest predictor of PTSD), rumination on the experience, poor 
social support 
Source. Haagen et al. 2015.

Intimate Partner Violence/Sexual Trauma Screening 
The ACOG promotes universal screening for IPV at the first prenatal visit, once per 
trimester, and at postpartum visits (American College of Obstetricians and Gynecol-
ogists 2012). All nonpregnant patients should be screened at every routine gynecolog-
ical visit, as well as at family planni ng and preconception vi sits. The USPSTF also 
recommends universal screening of all women of childbearing age (Curry et al. 2018).
