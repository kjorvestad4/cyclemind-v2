# Fertility & Reproductive Mental Health

> **Related:** [[Cycle-Tracking-Mental-Health]] · [[Perimenopause-Mental-Health]] · [[Perinatal-Depression]] · [[Trauma-Reproductive-Health]]

## Psychological Impact of Infertility
- **1 in 6 couples** globally affected by infertility
- Rates of depression and anxiety equivalent to those in serious medical illness
- Grief, loss of identity, relationship strain, sexual dysfunction all common
- Social isolation — "invisible grief" not always acknowledged

---

## Mental Health Across Fertility Treatment

### Pre-treatment
- Baseline mood screening recommended before IVF
- High baseline anxiety predicts more distress during treatment cycles
- Discuss realistic expectations and coping strategies

### During IVF / ART
- Anxiety peaks around oocyte retrieval and embryo transfer
- Depression increases with each failed cycle
- **Progesterone supplementation** during luteal support phase may affect mood
- **GnRH agonists** (for ovarian stimulation) can cause menopausal-like symptoms + mood effects

### After Failed Cycle / Negative Beta
- Anticipatory grief; acute grief response
- Normalize the need for time to process before next cycle
- Mental health support between cycles improves resilience and treatment adherence

### Pregnancy Loss (Miscarriage, Stillbirth)
- Grief is real and legitimate regardless of gestational age
- Up to 20% of recognized pregnancies end in miscarriage
- Rates of PTSD, depression, and anxiety significantly elevated after loss
- Provide immediate support resources; avoid dismissive language ("at least you know you can get pregnant")

## Primary Ovarian Insufficiency (POI)

### Definition
- Loss of normal ovarian function **before age 40**
- Diagnosed by elevated FSH (>25 IU/L) on two occasions ≥4 weeks apart + amenorrhea/oligomenorrhea

### Psychological Impact
- Sudden loss of fertility can be devastating
- Grief response; loss of expected life trajectory
- Compounded if concurrent cancer diagnosis (iatrogenic POI from chemotherapy)

### Physical Consequences Requiring Treatment
- **Estrogen deficiency:** Bone loss (osteoporosis risk), cardiovascular risk, cognitive concerns, GSM
- **MHT is recommended** until average age of menopause (~51) for women with POI
  - Dose typically higher than standard menopausal MHT
  - Protects bones, cardiovascular system, brain

### FMR1 Premutation & POI
- 12–28% of women with FMR1 premutation develop POI
- ACOG recommends FMR1 screening for all women with POI or elevated FSH <40

## Fertility Preservation

### Oncofertility
- Discussion of fertility preservation **before gonadotoxic treatment** is standard of care
- Options: oocyte cryopreservation, embryo freezing, ovarian tissue banking
- Urgency: can often start stimulation immediately (random-start protocols)
- Mental health support integral to oncofertility counseling

### Elective Egg Freezing
- Social egg freezing has grown significantly
- Address realistic success rates (declines with age)
- Discuss psychological implications of "banking" future fertility

---

## Pregnancy After Infertility
- **Higher anxiety** throughout pregnancy compared to naturally conceived pregnancies
- Increased monitoring may increase anxiety paradoxically
- Strategies: mindfulness, limiting Dr. Google, established mental health support
- Prior infertility does NOT increase PMDD or postpartum depression risk independent of age/trauma

## Ovarian Reserve Testing
| Test | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| **AMH** | Best marker of ovarian reserve; stable across cycle |
| **Early-cycle FSH** | Elevated = diminished reserve |
| **Antral Follicle Count (AFC)** | Ultrasound; correlates with retrievable eggs |
| **Estradiol day 3** | Elevated = suppressed FSH → falsely reassuring FSH |
