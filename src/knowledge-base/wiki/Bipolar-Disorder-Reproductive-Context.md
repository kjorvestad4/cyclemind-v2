# Bipolar Disorder in the Reproductive Context

> **Related:** [[Postpartum-Psychosis]] · [[PMDD]] · [[Perinatal-Depression]] · [[Perimenopause-Mental-Health]] · [[Schizophrenia-Reproductive-Context]]

---

## Overview
Bipolar disorder in women is profoundly shaped by reproductive hormonal transitions. The postpartum period carries the **highest relapse risk** of any phase of life for women with bipolar disorder.

---

## Relapse Risk Across Reproductive Life

| Phase | Risk Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Pregnancy | Moderate | Risk lower than postpartum but elevated vs. non-pregnant |
| Postpartum (first 4 weeks) | **Very High** | 37–67% relapse rate without prophylaxis |
| Perimenopause | Moderate-High | Estrogen fluctuation destabilizes mood |
| Menopause | Variable | Some stabilization; some worsening |

---

## Medication Management in Pregnancy

### Lithium
- **Teratogenicity:** Ebstein's anomaly risk — small absolute increase (~1–2 per 1000 vs 1 per 1000 baseline)
- **Renal clearance increases** during pregnancy → may need dose increases; monitor levels monthly
- **Toxicity risk peripartum** — clearance drops suddenly after delivery → reduce dose at delivery
- **Breastfeeding:** Compatible with monitoring; infant levels typically low

### Lamotrigine ⭐ Often Preferred for Bipolar Depression in Pregnancy
- Relatively favorable safety profile in pregnancy
- **Critical:** Clearance **increases significantly** during pregnancy (up to 3x) → frequent level monitoring and dose increases required
- Returns to pre-pregnancy clearance after delivery → dose must be reduced promptly to avoid toxicity
- Breastfeeding: Compatible; monitor infant

### Valproate — Generally AVOID in Pregnancy
- High teratogenicity: neural tube defects (1–2%), cardiac, craniofacial defects
- **Cognitive teratogenicity:** IQ reduction, autism spectrum risk in exposed offspring
- Use only when no safer alternative; contraception essential if reproductive-age

### Atypical Antipsychotics
- Increasing use in pregnancy for mood stabilization
- Quetiapine, olanzapine, risperidone have most pregnancy data
- Monitor for metabolic effects, gestational diabetes, neonatal adaptation syndrome

---

## Postpartum Management

### Prophylaxis (High-Risk Women)
- Initiate or restart mood stabilizer **immediately postpartum** (even if tapered during pregnancy)
- **Lithium** is often first-line for postpartum prophylaxis in bipolar I
- Sleep protection is critical — sleep deprivation is a major relapse trigger

### Monitoring
- Close psychiatric follow-up in first 2–4 weeks postpartum
- Educate family about early warning signs
- Low threshold to hospitalize if symptoms emerge — see [[Postpartum-Psychosis]]

---

## Perimenopause & Menopause
- Estrogen decline in perimenopause can destabilize bipolar disorder
- Increased frequency of depressive and mixed episodes common
- **Rapid cycling** may be triggered or exacerbated
- Consider adjunctive hormone therapy after careful risk-benefit analysis
- Antidepressants carry risk of mood destabilization; use with mood stabilizer

---

## Contraception Considerations
- **Lamotrigine + combined OCP:** Estrogen significantly reduces lamotrigine levels — increase dose when starting OCP; decrease when stopping
- **Valproate** reduces OCP efficacy — alternative contraception recommended
