# PMDD — Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder

> **Related:** [[Luteal-Phase-SSRI-Dosing]] · [[Cycle-Tracking-Mental-Health]] · [[Bipolar-Disorder-Reproductive-Context]] · [[Perinatal-Depression]]

---

## Definition
PMDD is a severe, cyclical mood disorder characterized by affective and somatic symptoms confined to the **late luteal phase** of the menstrual cycle that resolve within a few days after menses onset.

**Key distinction from PMS:** PMDD requires ≥5 symptoms (including ≥1 affective) with clinically significant distress or functional impairment, confirmed by prospective tracking over ≥2 cycles.

---

## DSM-5 Diagnostic Criteria

### Criterion A — Timing
Symptoms present in the **final week before menses**, begin to improve within a few days after menses onset, and are minimal or absent in the **follicular phase**.

### Criterion B — Core Affective Symptoms (≥1 required)
- Marked **affective lability** (mood swings, sudden sadness, tearfulness)
- Marked **irritability/anger** or increased interpersonal conflicts
- Marked **depressed mood**, feelings of hopelessness, or self-deprecating thoughts
- Marked **anxiety/tension**, feeling "keyed up" or "on edge"

### Criterion C — Additional Symptoms
- Decreased interest in usual activities
- Difficulty concentrating
- Lethargy, easy fatigability, marked lack of energy
- Marked change in appetite; food cravings or overeating
- Hypersomnia or insomnia
- Sense of being overwhelmed or out of control
- Physical symptoms: breast tenderness, bloating, joint/muscle pain, weight gain

### Criterion D — Severity
Symptoms cause **clinically significant distress** or interference with work, school, social activities, or relationships.

### Criterion E — Prospective Confirmation
Must be confirmed by **prospective daily ratings** for at least 2 symptomatic cycles. The **DRSP (Daily Record of Severity of Problems)** is the gold-standard validated tool.

---

## Epidemiology
- Affects **3–8%** of women of reproductive age
- Onset commonly in mid-to-late 20s; often worsens with age
- Elevated suicide risk — screen for suicidal ideation in all patients
- Strong familial aggregation; genetic component likely

---

## Pathophysiology
- PMDD is not caused by abnormal hormone levels but by **abnormal central sensitivity** to normal hormonal fluctuations
- Progesterone metabolite **allopregnanolone** modulates GABA-A receptors; dysregulation implicated
- Serotonergic system impairment in the luteal phase
- KNDy neuron involvement (shared with hot flash physiology)

---

## Diagnosis: Distinguishing PMDD from Other Conditions

| Condition | Key Differentiator |
|---|---|
| PMS | Fewer symptoms, less severe, no functional impairment required |
| Premenstrual Exacerbation (PME) | Symptoms present throughout cycle but worsen premenstrually |
| Major Depression | Symptoms not confined to luteal phase |
| Bipolar II | Hypomania in follicular phase is a clue |
| Perimenopause | Irregular cycles + vasomotor symptoms |

**Critical step:** Prospective charting over 2 cycles using DRSP separates PMDD from PME and other mood disorders.

## Treatment Algorithm

### First-Line
1. **SSRIs** — see [[Luteal-Phase-SSRI-Dosing]]
   - Sertraline 50–150 mg (daily or luteal-phase only)
   - Fluoxetine 20 mg (FDA-approved for PMDD as Sarafem)
   - Escitalopram 10–20 mg
2. **Non-pharmacologic** — see [[Integrative-Perinatal-Depression]]
   - CBT targeting symptom catastrophizing
   - Regular aerobic exercise (150 min/week)
   - Calcium 1200 mg/day (shown to reduce symptoms by ~30%)
   - Magnesium 200–360 mg/day (may reduce fluid retention, mood symptoms)

### Second-Line
- **OCP with drospirenone** (Yaz/Yasmin) — FDA-approved; suppresses ovulation
- **Continuous OCP** to eliminate the luteal phase entirely

### Third-Line (Severe/Refractory)
- **GnRH agonists** (e.g., leuprolide) with estrogen/progesterone add-back
  - Highly effective — creates temporary medical menopause
  - Add-back essential to prevent bone loss and vasomotor symptoms
- **Bilateral oophorectomy** — last resort for severe refractory cases
