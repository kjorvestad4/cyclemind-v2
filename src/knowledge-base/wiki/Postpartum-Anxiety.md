# Postpartum Anxiety Disorders

> **Related:** [[Postpartum-Depression]] · [[Postpartum-OCD]] · [[Anxiety-in-Pregnancy]] · [[Trauma-Reproductive-Health]] · [[Breastfeeding-Psychotropics]]

---

## Overview
Postpartum anxiety is **as common as — and often more common than — PPD**, yet receives far less clinical attention. Anxiety disorders affect up to **20%** of postpartum women.

---

## Types of Postpartum Anxiety

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) — Postpartum
- **Presentation:** Excessive, uncontrollable worry about infant health, safety, adequacy as a mother
- Hypervigilance — constant checking of infant breathing, color, temperature
- Physical: muscle tension, headaches, GI symptoms, insomnia
- Difficulty delegating infant care
- **Critical distinction from normal parenting:** Normal new-parent worry is proportionate and intermittent; GAD is persistent, excessive, and functionally impairing

### Panic Disorder — Postpartum
- Panic attacks may be triggered by: infant crying, night feeds, fear of SIDS, medical appointments
- Agoraphobia: avoidance of situations where panic might occur
- May avoid leaving the house, driving with baby
- Postpartum: often attributed to "new mom stress" and missed

### Postpartum PTSD (Birth Trauma)
- Estimated **9%** of postpartum women; higher in specific risk groups
- **Triggers:**
  - Emergency cesarean
  - Instrumental delivery (forceps, vacuum)
  - NICU admission
  - Life-threatening complication (hemorrhage, eclampsia)
  - Perceived lack of control or communication during delivery
  - Prior trauma (sexual abuse, previous traumatic birth)
- **Symptoms:**
  - Re-experiencing: flashbacks of delivery, intrusive images, nightmares
  - Avoidance: avoiding discussing birth, avoiding hospitals, avoidance of intimacy
  - Hyperarousal: startle response, sleep disturbance, irritability
  - Negative cognitions: "I failed," "My body betrayed me," "I nearly died"
- **Impact:** Avoidance of subsequent pregnancy; tokophobia; relationship disruption

### Postpartum OCD
- See [[Postpartum-OCD]] for full clinical detail
- Harm obsessions about baby: ego-dystonic, causes great distress
- Distinguish clearly from postpartum psychosis (insight intact vs. impaired)

### Specific Phobia — SIDS Fear
- Intense fear of sudden infant death leading to compulsive monitoring
- Baby monitors, sensor pads, frequent checking
- Sleep deprivation from checking; marital strain
- CBT targeting SIDS-related obsessional worry

---

## Why Postpartum Anxiety Is Under-Diagnosed
- Symptoms attributed to "normal new-mom worrying"
- Screening tools (EPDS) optimized for depression; miss anxiety
- Women feel they "should" be worried — normalizes pathological anxiety
- Shame: "I should be happy with a healthy baby"
- **EPDS Item 3, 4, 5** specifically tap anxiety — pay attention to these

---

## Screening Tools for Postpartum Anxiety
- **Generalized Anxiety Disorder Scale (GAD-7):** Score ≥10 = moderate-severe
- **Postpartum Specific Anxiety Scale (PSAS):** Validated specifically for postpartum anxiety
- **EPDS Anxiety Subscale (items 3–5):** Score ≥5 = elevated anxiety
- **Beck Anxiety Inventory:** Alternative validated tool

---

## Neurobiological Factors

### Estrogen Withdrawal
- Postpartum estrogen crash → serotonergic dysregulation → anxiety vulnerability
- Same mechanism as PPD; anxiety and depression often comorbid postpartum

### Sleep Deprivation
- Catastrophically amplifies anxiety; reduces prefrontal control of amygdala
- Hypervigilance worsens dramatically with sleep loss

### Lactation Hormones
- Oxytocin released during breastfeeding has anxiolytic effects
- Prolactin elevation has some calming effect
- Formula supplementation or abrupt weaning: oxytocin drop can worsen anxiety

## Treatment

### First-Line — Psychotherapy
- **CBT:** Worry control, decatastrophization, behavioral experiments ("what is the actual probability?")
- **EMDR:** For PTSD/birth trauma component
- **Birth trauma debriefing:** Structured narrative review of traumatic birth with midwife or therapist
- **Parent-infant CBT:** When hypervigilance is disrupting infant attachment

### Pharmacotherapy
- **SSRIs:** Sertraline preferred; begin low, titrate; allow 4–6 weeks for full effect
- **Buspirone:** Non-habit-forming; second-line; takes 2–4 weeks to work; safe in breastfeeding
- **Hydroxyzine:** For acute anxiety episodes; safe in breastfeeding; sedating
- **Benzodiazepines:** Sparingly; short-term; monitor infant; lorazepam preferred in breastfeeding
- See [[Breastfeeding-Psychotropics]] for detailed safety data

### Self-Management Strategies (Evidence-Based)
- Diaphragmatic breathing (4-7-8 technique)
- Progressive muscle relaxation
- Grounding techniques (5-4-3-2-1 sensory)
- Structured worry time (30 min/day; postpone worry outside this window)
- Sleep consolidation strategies (coordinated with partner feeding schedule)
- Limit Google health searches about baby symptoms

---

## Postpartum PTSD — Specific Interventions
- **EMDR:** Evidence-based for birth trauma PTSD
- **TF-CBT / CPT:** Trauma processing; cognition work about delivery
- **Narrative birth story work:** Helpful for some; re-processing the traumatic narrative
- Birth debrief with midwife: discuss what happened, why decisions were made — helps restore sense of coherence
- **Avoid:** Benzodiazepines as primary treatment for PTSD (does not treat underlying trauma)

---

## Partner/Support System Considerations
- Postpartum anxiety significantly affects the partner relationship
- Partner often becomes the "safety behavior" — checking because mother is too anxious
- Partner involvement in therapy beneficial
- Psychoeducation: explain that excessive reassurance maintains anxiety
