# Menopausal Hormone Therapy (MHT)

> **Related:** [[Perimenopause-Mental-Health]] · [[Cycle-Tracking-Mental-Health]] · [[Bipolar-Disorder-Reproductive-Context]]

---

## Overview
Menopausal hormone therapy (MHT) — formerly HRT — is the **most effective treatment** for vasomotor symptoms (hot flashes, night sweats) and genitourinary syndrome of menopause (GSM). For most healthy women under 60 or within 10 years of menopause onset, benefits outweigh risks.

---

## Who Should Consider MHT

### Indications
- Moderate-severe vasomotor symptoms impacting quality of life
- Genitourinary syndrome of menopause (vaginal dryness, dyspareunia)
- Prevention of bone loss in women at risk (osteopenia/osteoporosis)
- Mood symptoms in perimenopausal women (especially those with VMS-driven mood disruption)

### Absolute Contraindications
- Personal history of **breast cancer** (estrogen-sensitive)
- Personal history of **endometrial cancer** (unless progestin-protected)
- Active or recent **VTE** (DVT/PE)
- Active or recent **arterial thromboembolic event** (MI, stroke)
- **Undiagnosed vaginal bleeding**
- Active **liver disease**

---

## Components

### Estrogen
- **Systemic estrogen** — treats VMS, GSM, bone loss, mood
- **Preferred route:** Transdermal (patch, gel, spray) — lower VTE and stroke risk vs. oral
- **Transdermal estradiol** is bioidentical and preferred by most experts

### Progestin (required if uterus intact)
- **Micronized progesterone** (Prometrium) — bioidentical; preferred
  - Better cardiovascular and breast safety profile than synthetic progestins
  - 200 mg for 12 days/month (cyclic) or 100 mg daily (continuous)
- **Levonorgestrel IUD** (Mirena) as intrauterine progesterone alternative
- **Medroxyprogesterone acetate (MPA)** — synthetic; less preferred due to WHI data

### Estrogen Alone (hysterectomy only)
- No progestin needed; may have superior safety profile

---

## Regimens

| Regimen | Schedule | Bleeding Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Cyclic combined | Estrogen daily + progestin 10–14 days/month | Predictable monthly bleed |
| Continuous combined | Estrogen + progestin daily | No bleed (may have irregular spotting initially) |
| Sequential | Estrogen alone for first half of cycle, combined second half | Variable |

---

## Vaginal (Local) Estrogen for GSM
- **Ultra-low dose local estrogen** — cream, tablet, or ring
- Minimal systemic absorption; no progestin needed even with uterus
- Does NOT treat systemic VMS
- Safe for most women including many breast cancer survivors (discuss with oncologist)
- **Does not require progestin** even with intact uterus (ultra-low dose)

---

## Non-Hormonal Options for VMS
When MHT is contraindicated or declined:
| Option | Efficacy | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Venlafaxine/desvenlafaxine (SNRI) | Moderate | Also treats mood/anxiety |
| Escitalopram/paroxetine (SSRI) | Moderate | Paroxetine FDA-approved for VMS |
| Gabapentin | Moderate | Useful for nocturnal hot flashes/sleep |
| **Fezolinetant** (Veozah) | High | NK3 receptor antagonist; liver monitoring required |
| **Elinzanetant** | High | NK3R antagonist; also treats mood |
| Clonidine | Low | Limited efficacy; side effects |

---

## Duration of Therapy
- No absolute maximum duration — individualized
- Annual risk-benefit reassessment recommended
- Symptoms often recur on discontinuation — gradual taper vs. abrupt stop
- Many women need therapy for 7–10+ years due to persistent symptoms

---

## Compounded Bioidentical Hormones
⚠️ **Not recommended** by NAMS, Endocrine Society, or ACOG:
- Not FDA-regulated; variable potency and purity
- No evidence of superior efficacy or safety
- Transdermal estradiol + micronized progesterone ARE already bioidentical FDA-approved options

---

## Bone Health
- Estrogen prevents postmenopausal bone loss
- MHT reduces fracture risk (hip, spine, wrist)
- Not first-line for osteoporosis treatment if only bone protection needed
- DEXA scan baseline recommended at menopause or earlier if risk factors
