# Substance Use Disorders in the Perinatal Period

> **Related:** [[Perinatal-Depression]] · [[Trauma-Reproductive-Health]] · [[Pregnancy-Anxiety-Disorders]]

---

## Core Principle
**Substance use disorders are medical conditions** — not moral failures. Compassionate, non-judgmental, integrated care dramatically improves maternal and neonatal outcomes.

---

## Prevalence
- ~10–15% of pregnant women report substance use (all types)
- Underreported due to stigma and fear of legal consequences
- Screening must be universal, non-judgmental, and confidential

---

## Opioid Use Disorder (OUD) in Pregnancy

### Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) — Standard of Care
MAT is **superior to medically supervised withdrawal** (detox) in pregnancy:
- Reduces relapse and illicit opioid use
- Improves prenatal care engagement
- Reduces risk of overdose death
- Reduces preterm birth and low birth weight when patients are stabilized

### Buprenorphine (Subutex/Suboxone)
- **First-line option** for OUD in pregnancy
- Less fetal sedation than methadone
- Can be prescribed by certified outpatient providers
- Naloxone component: generally safe in pregnancy; some use buprenorphine monoproduct (Subutex)

### Methadone
- **Equally effective** to buprenorphine; gold standard since 1970s
- Must be dispensed daily through licensed opioid treatment programs (OTPs)
- More regulation but highly effective for women without stable housing or support
- Requires dose increases during pregnancy (increased volume of distribution)

### Neonatal Abstinence Syndrome (NAS)
- Expected with MAT — not a reason to avoid treatment
- **Non-pharmacologic first-line:** rooming-in, breastfeeding (if eligible), low-stimulation environment, skin-to-skin
- Pharmacologic (morphine, clonidine) when NAS is severe
- Mother on stable MAT: breastfeeding generally encouraged (low transfer, significant benefits)

## Alcohol Use in Pregnancy

### Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorders (FASD)
- **No safe level of alcohol** is established in pregnancy
- FASD is 100% preventable with abstinence
- Fetal effects: growth restriction, CNS damage, facial dysmorphia, intellectual disability
- Most severe form: Fetal Alcohol Syndrome (FAS)

### Screening
- AUDIT-C (brief validated screening)
- T-ACE, TWEAK validated for pregnancy

### Treatment
- Brief motivational counseling for mild-moderate use
- Formal addiction treatment for alcohol use disorder
- Naltrexone: avoid in pregnancy (limited safety data)
- Acamprosate: avoid (limited data)
- Thiamine replacement essential

---

## Cannabis Use in Pregnancy

### Risks
- Associated with: lower birth weight, preterm birth, fetal growth restriction
- **Potential long-term neurodevelopmental effects** on child cognitive function
- No safe level established — recommend cessation
- Often used for nausea — safer alternatives exist (doxylamine-B6, ondansetron)

### Patient Communication
- Many patients believe cannabis is "natural" and safe
- Motivational interviewing effective for reducing use

---

## Nicotine / Tobacco in Pregnancy

### Risks
- Preterm birth, low birth weight, placental abruption, placenta previa
- Stillbirth, sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS)
- **Quitting at any point in pregnancy helps**

### Cessation Support
- Behavioral counseling (individual + quitline: 1-800-QUIT-NOW)
- **Nicotine Replacement Therapy (NRT):** Generally preferred over continued smoking; risk-benefit discussion
- Varenicline (Chantix): limited pregnancy data; consider in severe dependence
- Bupropion: used in some cases; also treats depression

---

## Stimulants (Cocaine, Methamphetamine)
- Placental abruption, preterm birth, fetal growth restriction
- Maternal cardiovascular complications
- No FDA-approved pharmacotherapy
- Behavioral interventions + contingency management

---

## Benzodiazepines
- Short-term use may be necessary for severe anxiety/agitation
- Avoid prolonged use: neonatal sedation, withdrawal (floppy infant syndrome)
- Gradual taper if on chronic benzodiazepines — abrupt withdrawal dangerous

---

## Integrated Care Model
All women with perinatal SUD benefit from:
- OB care + addiction medicine + psychiatric care + social work
- Home visiting programs (Nurse-Family Partnership)
- Recovery support services (peer specialists, recovery coaching)
